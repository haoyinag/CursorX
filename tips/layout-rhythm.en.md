# Rhythm for layout switches

## When

- You jump often between code, terminal, Problems, and side bar
- Time goes to finding panels instead of work
- You want frequent UI switches to become habit

## Steps

1. Name your top three zones: editor, terminal/panel, side bar/file tree.
2. Give each switch a fixed entry point—side bar, bottom panel, terminal.
3. Keep a rhythm per session: fewer panels while coding; open Problems or terminal when debugging; open side bar when you need structure.

## What you get

- Less cognitive cost from constantly reshuffling layout
- Pays off in large repos and long tasks
- Easier to separate “focus mode” from “inspection mode”

## Pairs well with

- `toggle-sidebar-visibility`
- `toggle-panel`
- `toggle-terminal`
